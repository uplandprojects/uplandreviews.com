About the Product


Branded as the world’s first fully customizable front-loading upland hunting vest. It is completely adjustable and customizable to fit your hunting style. Just about everything on the Over/Under vest can be taken off or adjusted. Depending on how and where you hunt, you can either keep the vest streamlined and thin, or load it up with add-on's. These include ammo pouches, water bottle holder, electronics pouch, water blatter, and a gun stock rest.

All vests are meticulously crafted using 1000D CORDURA®, Hypalon and leather accents, rip-stop liners and every detail is stitched to perfection.

Vests and accessories can be ordered directly from the Chief Upland website.

Our Review Process


The first thing we noticed about this vest was the low profile. Unlike most other vests, it fits close to the body with an athletic style feel to it. We think this great when the weather is nice, but we are unsure how the vest might fit when we get bundled up for the cold weather season. So we decided to break up our review in two parts. One for when the weather is warm, and another for the late cold season. In this review, we will focus on how the vest performs in warmer weather.

What we found


Quality: The first thing that caught our eye was the unmatched quality. The stitching and materials are made to last season after season. You can tell that every inch of this vest has been planned out. It was built to ensure maximum comfort, durability, and the ability to customize. We have tested a lot of vests, the Chief Upland website Over/Under is by far the highest quality vest we have seen in it's price range.

Setup: Don't expect this vest to work for you right out of the box. The vest won't fit right without making alterations and adjustments. It requires some setup time to make sure it fits your body type and preferences. It's worth taking the time and getting right. Here are some tips to get you started. You can also watch this video on the Chief Upland YouTube channel.

Fit and feel: This vest fits differently compared to the others on the market. It has a slim profile and sits close to body. This athletic outline allows you to move through the north-woods with ease without getting hung up on debris. Once you get through the setup, the vest feels like it is "floating" on your waste. It also has lots of extra padding on the back for maximum comfort.

One flaw with the durable materials, extra back padding, and the athletic fit, is it makes this vest a bit uncomfortable on warm days. It is not breathable, so expect to sweat more with this vest during the early season.

Accessories: The Chief Upland Over/Under vest is pretty bare bones stock. It comes with a game bag and some small compartments in the back to hold a limited amount of gear. To make this vest usable in the field, you are going to need at least an ammo pouch. There are multiple accessories to choose from between a couple ammo pouches, water bottle holder, electronics pouch, and a gun stock rest. All the accessories are made with high quality, just like the vest. The vest system allows you to move the pouches around on the vest to match your personal hunting style.

Make sure you spend some time getting the accessory pouches setup to where you want them. It takes a few tries to get it to feel right.

The little things: It's the details of this vest that make it special. From the stitching, to the accessories, to the well placed cell phone compartment on the chest strap. You can clearly see that a lot of time and thought has gone into the design and manufacturing of this vest.

This innovative vest is the perfect profile for chasing birds in the midwest

You will not find another vest of this quality at this price point. In fact, you may not find another vest of this quality at all.

Setup Tips from Chief Upland
Chest Straps: The vest comes with two pairs (short & long). Use 1-2 straps depending on your needs, definitely not all 4. All 4 were just pre-installed for packaging purposes. Move/remove these as needed to find your comfort zone. Most people prefer one or two chest straps.

Shoulder Straps: You can move the attachment point on your hips. You don’t want it too far back that the shoulder straps are in your armpits. Find what’s comfortable. If you have the weight carrying properly on your hips, the shoulder straps almost just float along.

Waist Strap: Choose the higher or lower tunnel for the waist belt, depending on your height. Ideally, you want the waist belt running close to your navel. This helps the weight to carry on your hips, not your thighs or your shoulders. Some people still prefer the belt on their waist line which is fine too if that’s your comfort preference, this can be ok on shorter hunts, but on longer hunts this can fatigue your shoulders/thighs more.

Game bag/MOLLE panels: There is a small clip on the inside of the game bag on each hip. Clip the outer panel to the inner panel. This helps support the weight of the ammo attachments when full. Unclip it to font-load a bird, then clip it back on after loading a bird. The clip can also be moved forward or back, depending on where you place your attachments (if the weight is further forward or back).
