About the Product


Branded as the world’s first fully customizable front-loading upland hunting vest. It is fully adjustable and customizable to fit your hunting style. Just about everything on the Over/Under vest can be taken off or adjusted. Depending on how and where you hunt, you can either keep the vest streamlined and thin, or load it up with add-on's. These include two different ammo pouches, water bottle holder, electronics pouch, water blatter, and a gun stock rest.

All vests are meticulously crafted using 1000D CORDURA®, Hypalon and leather accents, rip-stop liners and every detail is stitched to perfection.

Vests and accessories can be ordered directly from the Chief Upland website.

Our Review Process


The first thing we noticed about this vest was the low profile. Unlike most other vests, it fits close to the body with an athletic style feel to it. We think this great when the weather is nice, but we are unsure how the vest might fit when we get bundled up for the cold weather season. So we decided to break up our review in two parts. One for the early and mid-season, and another for the late season. In this review, we will focus on how the vest performs in warmer weather.

What we found


Quality: The first thing you will notice is about this vest is the unmatched quality. The stitching and materials are made to last season after season. You can tell that every inch of this vest has been planned out to ensure maximum comfort, durability, and the ability to customize are achieved. We have tested a lot of vests, the Chief Upland website Over/Under is by far the highest quality vest we have seen in it's price range.

Setup: Don't expect this vest to work for you right out of the box. The vest won't fit right without making alterations and adjustments. It requires some setup to make sure it fits your body type and preferences. It's worth taking the time and getting right. Here are some tips to get you started. You can also watch this video on the Chief Upland YouTube channel.

Fit and feel: We do suggest that, unless you are going to specifically launch pheasants, you should go with the BL 509 series. The BL 709 is too much launcher for anything smaller. It becomes heavy to hall around the in the field. It's larger size also makes it difficult to hide in thin to moderate cover. The BL 509 is the right choice for most training situations.

Accessories: Our favorite feature with these launchers is how easy they are to load. The "easy-load" system provides an easy access door that is used to slip the bird into the launcher after it has been set. With the safety pin in place, you never have to worry about an accidental release.

That said the safety pin is secured by a small lightweight chain that tends to break easily. Sadly ours disappeared after only a couple weeks of use. We recommend securing it with something stronger than what is provided.

The little things:

Must have for anyone training young dogs

The BL 505 and the BL 705 are great bird launchers for both pro and amateur dog trainers. They are easy to use, safe, quiet, and dependable.

Setup Tips from Chief Upland
Chest Straps: The vest comes with two pairs (short & long). Use 1-2 straps depending on your needs, definitely not all 4. All 4 were just pre-installed for packaging purposes. Move/remove these as needed to find your comfort zone. Most people prefer one or two chest straps.

Shoulder Straps: You can move the attachment point on your hips. You don’t want it too far back that the shoulder straps are in your armpits. Find what’s comfortable. If you have the weight carrying properly on your hips, the shoulder straps almost just float along.

Waist Strap: Choose the higher or lower tunnel for the waist belt, depending on your height. Ideally, you want the waist belt running close to your navel. This helps the weight to carry on your hips, not your thighs or your shoulders. Some people still prefer the belt on their waist line which is fine too if that’s your comfort preference, this can be ok on shorter hunts, but on longer hunts this can fatigue your shoulders/thighs more.

Game bag/MOLLE panels: There is a small clip on the inside of the game bag on each hip. Clip the outer panel to the inner panel. This helps support the weight of the ammo attachments when full. Unclip it to font-load a bird, then clip it back on after loading a bird. The clip can also be moved forward or back, depending on where you place your attachments (if the weight is further forward or back).
