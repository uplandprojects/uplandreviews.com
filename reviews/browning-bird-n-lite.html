The Browning Bird'n Lite line is of the classic style of upland bird vests. They represent that traditional upland look you are used to seeing in the magazines and videos. These vests are made well and built to take plenty of abuse in the field. They can hold your gear, your birds, and will last season after season.

One of the things we like the most about this vest is that it fits like a vest should. Unlike other vests that fit more like a backpack or bag. It's comfortable, easy to get on and off, and easy to clean.

Browning carries two different options. The traditional vest or the strap vest. Both have advantages and disadvantages depending on what you are looking for.

Bird'n Lite 2.0

Browning has upgraded to a new generation of Bird'n Lite 2.0 vests. Although they look very similar, we have not had a chance to review the new line.

What we found


Durable: There is a reason the Bird'n Lite line has been around for years. The vest is made to last and holds up against heavy use, year after year.

Space: The traditional vest includes enough pockets and compartments for your shells, gear, and water bottles with room to spare. Having all that space comes at the price of being heavy. This makes the less than ideal in warmer weather. The Strap Vest on the other hand has far fewer compartments. It is lite weight and more comfortable during those early season afternoons.

Value: There are certianly cheeper vests out there. But the Browning Bird'n Lite line will give you the most vest for your dollars. With a starting price of only $150, it is great vest for anyone coming into the sport or who is on a budget.

Great vest for anyone starting out

This vest will last you for years. With a price of under $200, it's a great buy.
