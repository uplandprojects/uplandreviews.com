When we, as upland hunters, think of gear reviews we immediately envision guns, vests, electronic collars, boots, and all the other gadgets that make days afield with our four-legged friends more enjoyable. We recall the times when a blackberry bush made us reconsider our clothing choice, when a too-hot-to-handle 9 month old pup sent us shopping for a GPS collar, or when summer training routines had us considering which brand of bird launcher to invest in. The meticulous planning that precludes a successful fall is a year-round exercise that for many has become a way of life. Not unlike other equipment, training books and manuals hold a special place in the lives of upland hunters. While a hunting vest impacts our time in the field, training books can impact the larger portion of our life: time not in the field.

Most people in the upland hunting world have heard of the Smith family; the legacy of Delmar, Rick, Ronnie, and other Smiths penetrates hunting circles across the country. From East to West there are amateur and professional handlers watching “The Silent Command System” DVDs, attending seminars, and reading Training Bird Dogs with Ronnie Smith Kennels. As a unique blend of art and knowledge, the book is extremely detailed and covers the progressive training method the Smiths have been perfecting since the 1950s.

What we found


Quality Readers will immediately notice the size and quality of the hardback book; full sized, thick, photo quality paper makes the content in the book stand out from the crowd. The training method outlined by the book, often called the HuntSmith method, is a tried and true approach to training a pointing dog. The HuntSmith training method is centered around natural animal behavior, body language, and the fact that dogs don’t speak English/Spanish/French/German/etc. This training method develops dogs into strong, confident, cooperative, and proficient hunters.

An Easy Read A well detailed table of contents lays out the structure that takes the reader through a brief history of the Smith family of dog trainers, puppy development, formal training, and final polishing of a bird dog. The book includes practical, real world examples of where trainers often go wrong and actionable tips to avoid or correct these issues. The glossary at the end makes the book serviceable as a field guide, however the quality and imagery of the book make it more suitable as a coffee table staple that is sure to capture the imaginations of hunters and non-hunters alike.

Training Bird Dogs with Ronnie Smith Kennels is a must have for upland hunters who like to read or look at awesome pictures. The book is laid out sequentially and can be used as a quick reference for tackling nearly any training challenge you may find. Give this as a gift, buy it for yourself, or both and it will likely be the staple of a great upland book collection.
