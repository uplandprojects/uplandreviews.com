FieldTrainer or SportHunter?


SportDog offers two product lines that can be used for training upland dogs. The FieldTrainer and the SportHunter. Both are similar in basic functionality and yet provide enough differences to pay attention too. Each of the collars has at least 7 stimulation levels in 2 or more stim ranges. They include both vibration and tone options. Provide expandability to multiple dogs. And all are waterproof down to 25 feet. The biggest differentiator between models is the expanded range. The most difficult part of buying the collar is making the right choice that fits the needs of you and your dog.

Here is a matrix that highlights the major differences between each model. Details can be found on the SportDog website.

What we found


The variety of choices make it easy to find the collar right for your budget and your dog. Both FieldTrainers are simple to get started and use. Great for day to day use. The collars are water proof so they work well in all weather conditions. We use the FieldTrainer twice a day, every day. They stand up to daily use and are a reliable e-collar.

Longevity We noticed that after about 2 years the FieldTrainer starts to lose it's effectiveness. The range and stimulation decreases to the point where the collar is no longer usefull. Sadly, we need to purchase a new collar about every 2 years.

Picking the right collar For training and field use, you can't go wrong with either model. We use the FieldTrainer with our dogs every day. That said, taking into account the value and the price points, we would recommend going with FieldTrainer 425x or the SportHunter 825x. For most upland dogs the FieldTrainer 425S is too much collar. The only real value the SportHunter 1225 and 1825 brings is the additional range. If your dog is running that far out, an e-collar is not going to help you. Instead consider a GPS unit like the SportDog TEK Series or the Dogtra Pathfinder.

If you are looking for a collar that has audible tone, consider the UplandHunter 1875. It is the same collar as the SportHunter 1825, but includes an audible beeper that can be used for training and locating.
